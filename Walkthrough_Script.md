# RoloScan Dashboard — Walkthrough Script

---

## Opening

"Welcome to the RoloScan Dashboard. This is a live analytics view built on top of our scan and user activity data. It covers 60 organisations, over 1,500 users, and captures everything from how many business cards are being scanned each day to how productive individual sales reps are. The dashboard has four sections — let me walk you through each one."

---

## Tab 1 — Executive Summary

*Start here. This is the first thing anyone sees.*

"The Executive Summary is the 30,000-foot view. At the top you have five headline numbers — how many organisations we're tracking, total users, total leads added, calls made, and meetings scheduled across the entire platform."

**Search bar**
"If you want to zoom into a specific organisation, just type its name here and everything on this page — the numbers, both charts, and the table — updates instantly."

**Avg Engagement Score by Org**
"This chart ranks organisations by how active their users are on average each day. The engagement score is a weighted sum — meetings are worth the most because they take the most effort, followed by leads added, then calls, with emails and task completions at the baseline. Click the ℹ️ for the full breakdown. One thing to keep in mind — smaller orgs with just one or two users can appear unusually high or low here, so always glance at the Users column alongside it."

**Total Leads Added by Org**
"This shows raw pipeline contribution — how many new leads each org has added. Paragon Systems leads with 301. This is a good complement to the engagement chart because a high engagement score doesn't always mean the most leads — it's about activity quality versus volume."

**Full Org Summary Table**
"The table at the bottom pulls everything together in one place. You can search for any org and compare across conversion rate, task completion, confidence score, calls, emails, and meetings side by side. Hover over any column header with a ?" icon for a plain-English explanation of what that metric means."

---

## Tab 2 — Device & Location

*This tab is about who your users are, not what they do.*

"This section tells us about the people behind the numbers — where they are, what devices they use, and how long they've been on the platform."

**Users by Device Type**
"The split between Mobile, Desktop, and Tablet is almost even — about a third each. This tells us the product needs to work equally well across all screen sizes, and no single device type dominates."

**User Lifecycle Stages**
"This groups users by how long they've been signed up. The vast majority are in the Mature category — over 90 days on the platform. This dataset doesn't have any brand new users, which is important context when interpreting engagement scores — these are experienced users, so the bar is higher."

**Top 10 States by Users**
"Texas leads in user count, followed by other large states. This is useful for regional targeting — if you're planning outreach or events, these are the markets with the most presence."

**Operating System Breakdown**
"A mix of Android, iOS, Windows, and Mac. No single OS dominates, which again reinforces the need for cross-platform consistency."

**New User Signups Over Time**
"This goes back to May 2023 and shows when users first created their accounts. You can see which months had signup spikes — useful for understanding whether growth campaigns or product launches drove acquisition."

---

## Tab 3 — Org & Scan Performance

*This is the core product metric tab.*

"This tab focuses on the scanning side of the business — how many contacts are being scanned and how many of those turn into actual leads."

**KPI Cards**
"At a glance — 168,000 total scans in this period, an average conversion rate of 64.9%, and an average confidence score of 0.82. That confidence score means the AI is reading contact details correctly about 82% of the time with high certainty."

**Scan Trend — 7-Day Rolling Average**
"Rather than showing raw daily scans — which can spike and dip — this chart smooths the data over a 7-day window so you can see the actual trend. By default it shows two organisations. Use the dropdown above to add or swap in others. This is useful for comparing whether one org is growing their scanning activity while another is declining."

**Conversion Rate by Org**
"Not all scans lead to a new lead being created — this shows which orgs are best at turning a scan into a pipeline entry. Deeproot Analytics leads at 84%. A low conversion rate might mean users are scanning but not following through on creating the lead record in the system."

**Scans by Day of Week**
"This shows which days of the week see the most scanning activity. If there's a clear midweek peak, that's when the sales team is most active in the field — useful for scheduling training or sending nudges."

---

## Tab 4 — User Productivity

*This is the individual performance tab.*

"The final tab goes to the individual level — which users are driving the most activity, and how productive the team is on tasks and lead generation."

**Engagement Score Formula**
"As a reminder — the engagement score shown here is calculated as Meetings × 5, plus Leads Added × 4, Calls × 3, and Emails and Tasks each × 1. We deliberately excluded 'leads contacted' from the formula because calling someone and then logging a contact for the same interaction was double-counting effort."

**Top 15 Users by Total Engagement Score**
"Lisa Jackson from Mercer & Co tops the leaderboard. The bars are coloured by organisation so you can quickly see whether the top performers are spread across orgs or concentrated in one. This is useful for identifying which teams have standout individuals."

**Avg Daily Lead Velocity by Org**
"This converts weekly lead additions into a daily rate so orgs are comparable regardless of team size. It answers the question — on an average day, how many new leads is this org generating?"

---

## Closing

"Everything you see here updates automatically when the underlying data files are refreshed. The sidebar filters for organisation and month apply across all four tabs simultaneously, so you can focus in on a specific org or time period and every chart adjusts together. Happy to go deeper on any section."
